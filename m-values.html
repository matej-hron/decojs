üìä Understanding the M-Value Diagram

This chart plots Ambient Pressure (X-axis) against Tissue Inert Gas Pressure (Y-axis). The chart is divided into zones by key reference lines:

Key Lines

  • Alveolar Line (y = 0.79x) ‚Äî Where tissue pressure equals alveolar ppN‚ÇÇ (for air). Points above this line are off-gassing.
  • Ambient Line (y = x) ‚Äî Where tissue pressure equals ambient pressure. Points above this line are supersaturated.
  • M-Value Line ‚Äî Critical supersaturation limit:
    P_{tissue,tol} = a + \frac{P_{amb}}{b}
  • Surface Line (x = 1 bar) ‚Äî Sea level ambient pressure reference.

Chart Zones

Below Alveolar Line Undersaturated ‚Äî tissue still absorbing gas (on-gassing)
Between Alveolar & Ambient Off-gassing, not yet supersaturated
Between Ambient & M-Value Supersaturated but within tolerable limits
Above M-Value Line ‚ö†Ô∏è Critical supersaturation ‚Äî bubble formation risk!

Movement During a Dive

  • Descent: Move right (‚Üë ambient) and up (‚Üë tissue loading)
  • At depth: Stay at same X, slowly move up toward ambient line (saturation)
  • Ascent: Move left (‚Üì ambient), entering supersaturation zone

‚ö†Ô∏è Ascending too fast moves you left faster than you off-gas down ‚Äî risking crossing the M-value line!

üéöÔ∏è Gradient Factors (GF)

Gradient Factors are a way to add conservatism to the B√ºhlmann algorithm. Instead of allowing tissues to reach the raw M-value limit, we use only a fraction of the allowed supersaturation.

How GF Works

The GF-adjusted M-value is calculated as:

M_{adjusted} = P_{amb} + GF \times (M_{raw} - P_{amb})
  • GF = 100% ‚Äî Use full B√ºhlmann M-value (no conservatism)
  • GF = 70% ‚Äî Use only 70% of the allowed supersaturation
  • GF = 0% ‚Äî No supersaturation allowed (tissue must equal ambient)

GF Low / GF High

Two gradient factors control different phases of the ascent:

GF Low Applied at the first (deepest) deco stop. Controls how deep your first stop is.
GF High Applied at the surface. Controls how much supersaturation is allowed when you surface.
Between stops GF is linearly interpolated between GF Low at first stop and GF High at surface.

Common GF Settings

GF 100/100 Raw B√ºhlmann ‚Äî theoretical limits, not recommended for diving
GF 70/85 Moderate conservatism ‚Äî popular starting point
GF 50/80 More conservative ‚Äî deeper first stop, common for technical diving
GF 30/85 Very deep stops ‚Äî now considered potentially counterproductive

‚ö†Ô∏è Recent research (U.S. Navy, NEDU) suggests very low GF Low values may not reduce DCS risk and could increase it.
